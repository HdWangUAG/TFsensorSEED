## Claim / finding (1–3 bullets, with the numbers)
- AcrR drug-binding pocket = a ~350 Å³ internal cavity walled by helices α4–α8; predicted key contacts are hydrophobic **W63, I70, F114** plus electrostatic **E67 and Q130**, and the **E67A** mutation abolished binding of proflavin, ethidium and rhodamine 6G. 〔src: "Like other members of the TetR family, a large internal cavity, with a total volume of 350 Å3 is formed in the C-terminal region of AcrR (Fig. 1c)."〕 〔src: "The extensive binding pocket, which is created by helices α4 through α8, is mostly hydrophobic in nature, with W63, I70 and F114 predicted to make important hydrophobic contacts with the inducing ligands. In addition to these hydrophobic interactions, residues E67 and Q130 are predicted to make electrostatic interactions with bound drug to secure the binding."〕 〔src: "It was found that a mutation of this residue by an alanine, E67A, abolished the binding of Pf, Et and R6G to the regulator [12]."〕
- AcrR binds its inducers with affinities mirroring the AcrB pump: Et, Pf, R6G K_D = 4.2, 10.1, 10.7 μM (vs AcrB 8.7, 14.5, 5.5 μM); 1:1 ligand-to-monomer stoichiometry (TetR-like, not QacR's 1:2); binds operator IR as a dimer of dimers with K_D = 20.2 nM. 〔src: "Su et al. [22] demonstrated that AcrR binds ethidium bromide (Et), proﬂavin (Pf), and rhodamine 6G (R6G) with dissociation constants of 4.2, 10.1, and 10.7 μM [22]; while the KD values for AcrB with these ligands are 8.7, 14.5, and 5.5 μM [26], respectively."〕 〔src: "Each AcrR monomer binds an inducing ligand, and thus a dimer can accommodate two identical molecules."〕 〔src: "It has been demonstrated through ﬂuorescence polarization and gel ﬁltration that AcrR binds to this IR as a dimer of dimers, with a dissociation constant (KD) of 20.2 nM [12,22]."〕
- Allosteric mechanism: two crystal forms (P2221 "drug-bound-like" vs P31 "DNA-bound-like") differ mainly at the N-terminal domain (rmsd 2.8 Å vs <0.7 Å for C-terminal); **E67 acts as a molecular switch** (Cα shifts 4.2 Å), and an interdomain H-bond network (E67–R106, R105–Q14, R105–D18) present in P2221 is lost in P31; recognition-helix center-to-center (Y49–Y49′) goes 42→39 Å between forms (must reach ~34 Å to bind B-DNA). 〔src: "This change results in an overall rmsd of 2.8 Å calculated over the Cα atoms at the N-terminal domains (residues 7–65), in contrast to the b0.7 Å rmsd of the C-terminal domains (residues 73–210) [13]."〕 〔src: "the most striking conformational change involves the amino acid E67. This residue may act as a molecular switch that drives the change of conformations throughout the AcrR molecule. Superimposition of the two AcrR structures reveals that the Cα atom of E67 shifts by 4.2 Å."〕 〔src: "The center-to-center distance between recognition helices α3 and α3′ (as measured by the distance between Cα atoms of Y49 and Y49′) decreases from 42 Å in the P2221 structure to 39 Å in the P31 structure [13]."〕

## Why it matters to TFsensor
- Gives the explicit AcrR pocket residue list to target for steroid redesign — hydrophobic shell (W63/I70/F114) for a steroid core plus tunable polar anchors (E67/Q130) — and shows E67 is functionally load-bearing (E67A kills binding), so it is a residue to mutate cautiously / map carefully. 〔src: "with W63, I70 and F114 predicted to make important hydrophobic contacts with the inducing ligands... residues E67 and Q130 are predicted to make electrostatic interactions"〕
- Provides a concrete allostery model to encode in scoring: drug binding flips E67 inward, forms an interdomain H-bond network, and pushes recognition helices apart (closing/opening DNA affinity) — i.e. selectivity must couple to the E67/R105/R106 switch, not just pocket fit. 〔src: "Drug-binding within the C-terminal domain may induce structural changes resulting in a conformation more closely related to the P2221 structure, in which the side chain of E67 ﬂips into the interior of the hydrophobic core. This change may also be accompanied by the formation of new H-bonds between E67 and R106, R105 and Q14, and R105 and D18."〕
- Sets baseline affinity/charge expectations: AcrR is induced by cationic/neutral ligands with low-μM K_D and 1:1 monomer stoichiometry — useful anchors for a neutral-steroid target and for sensitivity/dynamic-range design. 〔src: "Induction of AcrR is initiated through an interaction of cationic and neutral ligands."〕 〔src: "The KDs in this range may be optimal to initiate the expression of MDR pumps while the antibiotic concentration is at the sub-inhibitory level."〕

## Applicability caveats
- Pocket residues (W63, E67, I70, F114, Q130) and ligand poses come from docking/binding-site prediction, not from a drug-bound AcrR co-crystal; the authors note both DNA-bound and drug-bound structures are still needed to confirm the mechanism. 〔src: "The crystal structures of both DNA-bound and drug-bound AcrR would be necessary to conﬁrm the change in conformation upon DNA and drug binding."〕
- Characterized ligands are dyes/antibiotics (ethidium, proflavin, R6G, ciprofloxacin), not steroids; the cavity is small (~350 Å³) and AcrR favors cationic/neutral ligands — so transfer to a (neutral, bulky) steroid likely requires cavity enlargement and is not directly evidenced here. 〔src: "Docking of ligands into the AcrR structure suggested that the large cavity created by helices α4 to α8 could accommodate different drugs, including Et, Pf and ciproﬂoxacin (Cip) [12]."〕
- This is a secondary review; quantitative values originate in primary papers [12] (Li et al., J. Mol. Biol. 2007), [13] (Gu et al., Acta Cryst. F 2008) and [22] (Su et al., BBRC 2007) and should be verified there before use. Much CmeR-specific detail (E. coli vs C. jejuni) is a different organism/regulator and only loosely informs AcrR. 〔src: "we determined the crystal structures of the AcrR and CmeR regulatory proteins. In this review, we will summarize the new ﬁndings with AcrR and CmeR"〕
